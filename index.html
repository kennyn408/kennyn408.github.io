Presenting 12 Various DMA Projects

1. Augmented Glitch Art

I compiled six photographs, all of which I took myself, into a digital collage with Adobe Photoshop. Then I added a glitch effect using Audacity. This glitch aspect is better viewed in the Blipp AR app.


2. 3D Mashup of OBJ's

Using Meshmixer, I implemented dogs, an elephant, and a gremlin who are all playing with a ball in the middle, like they are in a playground for animals.


3. Video & Sound Art

This project was a group project, consisting of 3 group members: myself, Jigme Farber and Ramon Franco. Ramon casually enters the museum and examines several art galleries in the San Jose Museum of Art that evoke a sense of PTSD.


4. Net Art Animation

Using three or four snippets on ThreeGUI - WebGL Renderer, I created a successful animation of a star-shaped object rotating, moving forward and backward, and alternating background colors.


5. Processing Avatar

I created this avatar image of myself through Processing. I used various shapes, such as semi-circles, ellipses and rectangles, to represent my glasses, hat, and facial features.


6. Processing Animation

Being an avid Marvel fan, I created an animation with Processing, consisting of Captain America’s shield, Iron Man’s helmet, and the Avengers logo. The animation includes a mouse interaction, where red circles on the screen widen or lengthen depending on the cursor's movement.


7. Plywood Tiny Box

Utilizing disc sanders, band saws, and a few other woodshop machines, I built a miniature wooden drawer made of plywood. The drawer box is contained in a bigger box and can slide in and out of the box.


8. Editing in Premiere

Primarily using Ultra Key effects in Adobe Premiere Pro, I edited a video to tell a story of 2 competitive soccer players, me and my friend, playing a game of soccer. The game eventually escalates to a game where they both cheat, each to a greater degree after each goal because they were focused on performing better than the other.


9. Avatar/Data Self Portrait

With p5.js, I created an avatar resembling a watch face that depicts the message: My childhood has passed faster than I would have liked and I wish I could go back to being having a worry-free and fun childhood. The sketch has mouse interaction, microphone input, rotating minute hand, and a group of dots rotating continuously.


10. Never Seen, Never Will

This was a project that I created that consist of a miniature version of an object, place, or concept that I have never seen in real life, nor will I ever see. That object is a monument called the Eiffel Tower, located in Paris, France. This tower is made entirely of cardboard and has a black and white gooey texture printed on copy paper. A platform was made for the tower as well and can be detached from the tower.


11. Let It Ring

Using a variety of woodworking machines, including bandsaws, sanders, and drill presses, I built a bell with the capability to ring. The bell has a cylindrical shape and can be hung on a hook.


12. Play Project - Skate Park

I created a miniature skate park to evoke the concept of “Play” using primarily cardboard. Within the skate park, I demonstrated wood joinery and connective systems that can be achieved with a bandsaw. Because of the COVID-19 pandemic, we had no access to a bandsaw this semester but I still demonstrated proficiency in preparing a model for the bandsaw.
